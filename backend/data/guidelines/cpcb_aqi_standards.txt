Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) National Ambient Air Quality Standards and AQI Categories (Source: Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change, Government of India)
Air Quality Index (AQI) values correspond to six specific health advisory categories:
1. Good (AQI 0 - 50): Minimal health impact. Safe for all outdoor activities.
2. Satisfactory (AQI 51 - 100): May cause minor breathing discomfort to sensitive people.
3. Moderate (AQI 101 - 200): May cause breathing discomfort to people with lungs, asthma, and heart diseases.
4. Poor (AQI 201 - 300): May cause breathing discomfort to most people on prolonged exposure. Sensitive groups should avoid heavy outdoor exertion.
5. Very Poor (AQI 301 - 400): May cause respiratory illness on prolonged exposure. Effect may be more pronounced in people with lung and heart diseases. Active children and adults should avoid outdoor activities.
6. Severe (AQI 401 - 500): Affects healthy people and seriously impacts those with existing diseases. Outdoor physical exertion should be completely avoided.

Safe exposure limits for primary pollutants over a 24-hour average:
- PM2.5 (Particulate Matter < 2.5 microns): 60 µg/m³
- PM10 (Particulate Matter < 10 microns): 100 µg/m³
- NO2 (Nitrogen Dioxide): 80 µg/m³
- SO2 (Sulfur Dioxide): 80 µg/m³
- O3 (Ozone, 8-hour average): 100 µg/m³
If PM2.5 averages above 60 µg/m³, it exceeds the standard limit, triggering health advisory flags.
